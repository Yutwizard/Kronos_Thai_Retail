1. Walk-Forward Architecture

Every backtest is a strict walk-forward simulation: forecasts for day t use only data available through day t, and trades execute at the t+1 open price. No look-ahead.

3. Friction Model

Costs applied on every buy and sell. Defined in kth/data/universe.py as FRICTION.

Crypto (BTC only)0.25%0.10%0.70%

Impact: 11.8× annual turnover × 0.536% = 6.3% of AUM per year consumed by friction. CAGR of +31.44% is net of these costs. Gross returns were ~+38% before friction.

Worked example: Buy 2,000 shares of PTT.BK at 32.50 THB = 65,000 THB. Commission: 65,000 × 0.168% = 109.2 THB. Slippage: 65,000 × 0.10% = 65.0 THB. One-way: 174.2 THB. Round-trip: 348.4 THB (0.536%).

6. Known Limitations

  • Only 3 of 9 classes backtested. Thai equity, US equity, and crypto have walk-forward backtests. ETF global, commodity, bond, REIT, FX are untested. Half the universe has no backtest validation.
  • Survivorship bias. The 49 Thai equity tickers were selected as of mid-2025. Thai stocks delisted, suspended, or merged between 2020 and 2024 are not included because yfinance does not expose historical data for delisted securities.

    Academic studies of SET delisting rates (2010–2020) suggest survivorship bias inflates reported CAGR by approximately 1–3 percentage points per year for universe sizes comparable to ours (40–60 stocks). Applying this range to our 2022–2024 result of +31.44% CAGR, the survivorship-adjusted estimate is approximately +28–30% CAGR.1 The qualitative conclusion — genuine alpha over SET and Equal-Weight benchmarks — is robust to this adjustment.

    Mitigation path: A point-in-time universe (using SET constituent lists from 2020 onwards) would eliminate this bias but requires historical sector data not available via yfinance. Flagged as a future enhancement.
  • Free data quality. Yahoo Thai stock prices can have stale data, gaps, and bad ticks. Extreme moves are flagged but not corrected.
  • No capacity constraints. Assumes ~200K THB positions fill at open. Real large orders could move the market.
  • Tax not modelled. Thai gains are taxed as personal income (0-35%). Net of tax, returns would be lower.
  • Short time window. 3 years (2022-2024) is one macro regime. A different 3-year window would produce different results.
